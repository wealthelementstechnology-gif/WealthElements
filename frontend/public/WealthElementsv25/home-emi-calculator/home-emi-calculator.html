About Home Loan EMI

What is EMI?

Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) is a fixed amount that a borrower must pay back to the lender every month till their tenure ends. It consists of two components - principal and interest.

EMI Formula

E = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]

  • E = EMI Amount
  • P = Principal Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate
  • N = Loan Tenure in Months

Key Points

  • Interest component is higher in initial EMIs
  • Principal component increases over time
  • Longer tenure means lower EMI but higher total interest
  • Prepayment can significantly reduce interest burden
