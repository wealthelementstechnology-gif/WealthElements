# WealthElements AI — India-Specific Knowledge Base

## Property & Real Estate

- Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad property price ranges and market realities
- SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis, Kotak home loan rates (currently ~8.5-9.5% floating)
- Stamp duty by state: Maharashtra 5-6%, Karnataka 5%, Delhi 4-6%, Telangana 4%
- Registration charges: typically 1% of property value (capped in some states)
- Under-construction vs ready-to-move trade-offs: GST on under-construction (5%), no GST on ready

## Tax Rules

- Section 80C: ₹1.5L limit (EPF, ELSS, PPF, life insurance premium, home loan principal)
- Section 80D: health insurance premium deduction (₹25K self, ₹50K senior parents)
- Section 24: home loan interest deduction up to ₹2L/year (self-occupied)
- HRA exemption: min of (actual HRA, 50%/40% of basic for metro/non-metro, actual rent - 10% of basic)
- New vs Old regime: compare carefully — new regime removes most deductions but has lower slab rates
- LTCG: 10% on equity gains above ₹1L (held 1+ year), no indexation benefit
- STCG: 15% on equity gains (held under 1 year)

## Investment Instruments

- PPF: 15-year lock-in, 7.1% current rate, partial withdrawal from year 7, EEE status
- NPS: Tier 1 (locked till 60, extra ₹50K deduction under 80CCD(1B)), Tier 2 (flexible, no lock-in)
- ELSS: 3-year lock-in, market-linked, counts for 80C
- SGB (Sovereign Gold Bond): 2.5% annual interest + gold price appreciation, 8-year tenure
- FD rates: typically 6.5-7.5% at major banks (2026), taxed as income
- RD vs SIP: RD is fixed return, SIP is market-linked — for long goals, SIP wins inflation-adjusted

## EPFO / EPF

- EPF transfer on job change: use UAN, file online claim
- Partial withdrawal conditions: medical, housing, marriage, education (conditions apply)
- Employer contributes 12% of basic to EPF; employee matches; 8.33% goes to EPS
- Withdrawal before 5 years: taxable; after 5 years: tax-free

## Credit & Loans

- Credit card interest: 36-42% per annum — highest-cost debt, always clear first
- Personal loan rates: 12-24% depending on bank and credit score
- Home loan prepayment: no penalty for floating rate loans (RBI mandate)
- EMI-to-income safe threshold: 40% maximum; below 30% is comfortable

## Indian Spending Patterns to Watch

- Zepto/Blinkit: impulse buys, convenience premium adding up
- Swiggy/Zomato: food delivery lifestyle inflation — track weekly
- Subscription creep: Netflix, Prime, Hotstar, Spotify, gym — audit quarterly
- Gold buying: cultural habit, but compare with SGB for same exposure with yield

## Middle-Class Financial Pressures

- Parents expecting financial support: factor as a recurring expense, not surprise
- Weddings as financial events: ₹5L-₹50L range — plan 3-5 years ahead
- Property as social status: separate need from want before committing
- Gold as savings: valid culturally, but diversify into financial assets too
- Job stability anxiety: emergency fund is the #1 buffer — never deprioritize it

## SIP & Mutual Fund Platforms

- Groww, Zerodha Coin, MF Central, CAMS, ET Money
- Direct vs Regular plans: direct saves ~0.5-1% expense ratio per year — significant over 10+ years
- Index funds vs active: for large-cap, index often wins after costs; mid/small-cap active has more edge
